Papercrafted Aventures is a game that my friends and I made a couple years ago. Here are the rules. Enjoy.

Rules

General Rules

  • A game is played with a host and at least 2 players.
    • The host can be a player; it is up to the player’s choice.
  • A map is drawn.
  • Each player decides on a symbol.
    • The location of the symbol on the map corresponds to the location of the player.
  • Each player has an inventory and a stat menu.
    • All players are conscious by necessity at the start of the game.
  • Mods can be added to the game, but they should be consistent and used for the entirety of the game.
    • An example of a mod is a scale for describing the importance of wounds.
    • This includes modifications to the rules to account for a different game medium.
  • Each player inputs their turn and gets one roll per turn. The roll decides their success at the action described in their turn.
    • Turns happen simultaneously, so all of the rolls and inputs must be in before deciding on an effect if necessary. See 1.2, 1.3
  • The host describes what happens and modifies the state of the game based on the current game, roll, and input.
    • It’s okay for a player to ask a host to clarify something, unless otherwise specified.
  • The last player (or team) to have their consciousness destroyed wins.
    • A sleeping player or KOd player can win, but a killed one cannot. If conscious is recovered, a player can not regain their ability to win.

Rolling

  • Use an eraser with angled edges.
    • "Pink Eraser, Erases Clean and Clear"
  • The largest sides are considered "up" and "down."
    • The marked side is considered up by convention.
  • The thinner sides are considered "good side" and "bad side."
    • If the eraser lands on these at any time during rolling, the roll counts as the corresponding side.
    • If it lands on more than one good or bad side, good sides trump.
    • It would be really cool to get more than one good side a roll.Sand staffs are realy cool :A -Scarce (IO)
  • If you don't have a standard eraser, adjust the value of a side based on the thickness of the eraser.
  • The game is biased towards success in order to promote the propagation of shenanigans.
  • If the first roll is DOWN, the value is 3. If the first roll is UP, the value is 4. In the subsequent 2 rolls, add or subtract one when you roll an UP or DOWN, respectively. This is the final value.

Roll Values

  • "Bad side" is the worst roll, often severely damaging the player who rolls it. It doesn’t often kill, but hurts like hell.
  • 1 is the worst normal roll. Like a bad side, but less severe, and can often be recovered from with a better roll on the next turn
  • 2 is normally a minor inconvenience that doesn’t do much to the player who rolls it. It can be damaging if the player is doing something very important.
  • 3 is completely neutral. Rolling a 3 can accomplish basic tasks like walking and other stuff like pooping.
  • 4 is slightly positive. A bit of good luck, like finding a lucky penny, but more useful.
  • 5 is good. It won’t make player god, but often tasks will be completed well.
  • 6 is very lucky. It can get someone a much greater reward for a turn than other rolls. Multiple 6s can go a long way
  • "Good side" is the best roll. Getting one can get a player damn near anything, from an epic sword to full healing to a rod that controls sand in the middle of a freaking desert.
    • They make the game memorable.

Team Rules

  • There can be more than one winner if players form a team and survive.
    • In order to join or create a team, members must participate in some form of in-game communication.
    • A player cannot win by losing but being on a winning team.
  • An NPC ally can not win the game for a lost player, even if the player can still control them.
  • If all the remaining players die in the same turn, it is a tie between those players. A player must last a turn greater than other players in order to win.
